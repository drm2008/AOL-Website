PART 5: Data Analysis - Statistical Foundations

Univariate

Analysis of a single variable. (e.g., Mean, Histograms, Standard Deviation).

Bivariate

Relationship between two variables. (e.g., Scatter Plots, Pearson's Correlation).

Multivariate

Relationship among multiple variables (e.g., Regression, MANOVA, SEM).

Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics involves methods used to summarize and describe important characteristics or features of a dataset. Descriptive statistics include measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode), measures of dispersion (variance, standard deviation), and frequency distributions. Descriptive statistics are useful for understanding the basic properties of data, identifying patterns, detecting outliers, and making initial interpretations before applying more advanced statistical techniques.

Key Applications

  • Business/Market Research: Customer behavior and segmentation.
  • Healthcare: Patient history and epidemiological tracking.
  • Education: Assessment performance analysis.
  • Quality Control: Monitoring manufacturing variation.

Inferential Statistics

Parametric statistical tests are those that assume the data come from a population that follows a specific distribution, typically the normal distribution. They rely on parameters such as the mean and standard deviation and generally use interval or ratio scale data.

Parametric Tests

Parametric statistical tests are those that assume the data come from a population that follows a specific distribution, typically the normal distribution. They rely on parameters such as the mean and standard deviation and generally use interval or ratio scale data. They require normality, homogeneity of variance.

Examples: t-test, ANOVA, ANCOVA, MANOVA, Linear Regression, Multiple Regression.

Nonparametric Tests

Nonparametric statistical tests are methods used when data do not meet the assumptions required for parametric tests, such as normal distribution or interval-level measurement. They do not assume any specific population distribution and are often based on ranks or signs rather than raw data values.

Examples: Mann-Whitney U, Wilcoxon, Kruskal-Wallis, Chi-Square.

Normality & Linearity

Normality Checks

  • Shapiro-Wilk (Small samples)
  • Kolmogorov-Smirnov (Larger samples)
  • Anderson-Darling

Linearity Checks

  • Harvey-Collier Test
  • CUSUM Test
  • Scatterplots/Residual plots
